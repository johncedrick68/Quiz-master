CHAPTER 1 



INTRODUCTION



Rationale of the Study



	Public transportation is a fundamental driver of economic activity and social mobility in rapidly urbanizing environments. However, navigating complex transit networks often imposes significant "transit anxiety" on passengers, characterized by a fear of missing transfers, becoming lost, or overshooting a destination. Research on mental health and travel indicates that nearly half of passengers experience anxiety related to wayfinding, while 46% report stress specifically when required to interact with transit staff or drivers. The absence of understandable transit data creates a significant cognitive load for those new to a city, say urban mobility specialists at the United Nations Human Settlements Programme (UN-Habitat). In addition, transport economists from the World Bank point out that the usual apps, such as Google Maps, do not take into account informal paratransit networks, leaving unknowledgeable commuters completely stranded. Transportation scholars Klopp and Cavoli (2019) argue that informal paratransit networks exist in a "digital blind spot," as mainstream navigation applications often fail to map these systems, forcing commuters into a stressful trial-and-error process to identify boarding points and correct vehicles 



	In the Philippines, this information gap is particularly acute within the public utility jeepney (PUJ) sector. While the Department of Transportation (DOTr) has prioritized physical fleet upgrades through modernization programs, the availability of digital passenger information remains negligible. Dr. Sheilah Napalang of the Philippine Institute for Development Studies (PIDSx`) identifies a structural "information asymmetry" where critical data on boarding zones and distance-based fare structures are often limited to local knowledge. This gap creates a significant barrier for "shy communicators" or passengers with social anxiety who hesitate to ask strangers for directions or clarify changing fares, often leading to navigational errors or overpayment. (Zulueta et al., 2024; Manguan et al., 2026).



	This crisis bottlenecks completely in Cebu City, where the Cebu City Transportation Office (CCTO) and the Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board (LTFRB) Region VII manage a complex alphanumeric routing network (e.g., 04L, 13C) that is entirely unintelligible to newcomers. Regional Director Eduardo Montealto Jr. has noted that a total lack of street-level signage leaves clueless commuters blind regarding which specific jeepney to take, where to wait, and how to find the nearest boarding zone from their present location. Furthermore, once inside the vehicle, passengers have absolute spatial blindness; without visual or auditory route cues, they remain completely clueless about when or where to signal the driver to stop ("Para") or if they have already arrived at their destination, trapping them in a state of constant panic.

	





   





Abstract

Public utility jeepneys remain one of the primary modes of transportation in Cebu City, yet many commuters experience difficulties in identifying the correct routes, estimating fares, and navigating unfamiliar destinations. This study developed PasaHERO, a web-based commuter assistance application that integrates multimodal AI navigation, jeepney route visualization, and fare analytics to provide a more convenient and efficient commuting experience. The system features AI-powered route recommendations, interactive map visualization, landmark-based guidance, first-mile and last-mile navigation, automated fare estimation based on LTFRB rates, and geofenced "Para" notifications to assist commuters during their trips. Using a quantitative research approach, data were gathered through a structured survey questionnaire administered to selected jeepney commuters in Cebu City to identify their commuting challenges and evaluate the proposed system's functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and user satisfaction. The findings of the study are expected to contribute to the improvement of intelligent transportation systems by providing an accessible, user-friendly, and AI-driven navigation platform that enhances the overall public commuting experience in Cebu City.

Scope and Limitations 



Scope of the Study

Public transportation is essential for daily commuting, particularly in highly congested urban areas such as Cebu City. The proposed system, PasaHERO, is designed to assist commuters by generating accurate and efficient jeepney routes, providing fare estimates, and offering clear, landmark-based guidance throughout each trip. 



Scope of the Proposed System

Users must be registered to access and use the web application.

The system records essential commuter profile information (such as age, gender, educational background, public transportation usage, familiarity with routes, and prior application experience) for research and analytical purposes, with all personal data handled confidentially and in compliance with relevant data privacy regulations.

The system shows specific jeepney routes and signboards (e.g., 04L, 13C) that the commuter should ride to reach a chosen destination.

The system visualizes trips through a color‑coded map, using a red line for the active jeepney/public transport path and a green line for walking paths (first‑mile and last‑mile segments).

The system provides estimated fares based on distance and current LTFRB distance‑based fare rates, distinguishing between traditional and modernized jeepneys.

The system highlights significant landmarks near boarding and drop‑off points to guide users who are unfamiliar with the area.

The system sends geofenced “Para” notifications (visual alerts and/or vibration) when the user approaches the designated drop‑off point.

The system provides first‑mile and last‑mile walking directions between the user’s current location, jeepney stops, and final destination.

The study evaluates the accuracy of route and fare recommendations, clarity of route visualization, usability, and overall user satisfaction for selected jeepney routes in Cebu City.





Limitations of the Proposed System

The application is available exclusively as a web‑based system and must be accessed via an internet‑enabled device (e.g., smartphone, tablet, or laptop) with a modern web browser.

A stable internet connection and access to location services (such as GPS or equivalent) are required for route generation and geofenced alerts.

The application supports only jeepney‑based public transportation within selected areas and routes in Cebu City; other modes such as buses, taxis, ride‑hailing services, and ferries are not included.

The system does not provide real‑time jeepney locations or arrival times and relies on predefined jeepney routes and stops.

The fare calculator is limited to standard LTFRB distance‑based rates for traditional and modernized jeepneys and may not automatically compute discounts for students, senior citizens, persons with disabilities, or other special fare arrangements.

The application does not process or handle online payments; all payments are made directly to the driver or conductor, with the fare estimate serving only as a guide.

The system may not immediately reflect sudden changes such as temporary road closures, rerouting, strikes, or unannounced modifications to jeepney routes or fare policies.

The evaluation of the system is limited to a selected group of participants within a defined data collection period and may not fully represent the wider commuting population in Cebu City or in other locations.

The application does not support booking or reserving jeepney seats and is limited to navigation, route guidance, and fare estimation.

The system is implemented and evaluated as a prototype for academic research and may not yet include all features and robustness required for large‑scale commercial deployment.

Definition of Terms



Artificial Intelligence (AI): refers to the algorithms in PasaHERO that process location and transport data to suggest routes and compute fares.



Multimodal Navigation: is guiding a commuter using both walking and jeepney segments in a single trip.



Jeepney: is a public utility vehicle in the Philippines that follows fixed routes and charges distance-based fares.



Jeepney Route Code (e.g., 04L, 13C): is the alphanumeric label that identifies a specific jeepney route in Cebu City.



Signboard: is the route text displayed on a jeepney (e.g., “Colon – Banilad – Talamban”) that shows its main destinations.



Route Visualization: is the display of walking and jeepney paths as lines on a digital map.



Color-Coded Route Overlay: is the map style where red lines show jeepney routes and green lines show walking paths.



First-Mile/Last-Mile Guidance: is navigation from the user’s exact location to the jeepney stop, and from drop-off to the final destination.



Geofenced “Para” Notification: is an alert triggered when the user is near their planned drop-off point.



Dynamic Fare Calculator: is the feature that estimates jeepney fare based on distance and current fare rules.



Landmark-Based Guidance: uses nearby buildings or notable places to help users confirm the correct stop or waiting point.



LTFRB Fare Matrix: is the official fare schedule used as basis for computing jeepney fares in the system.



Cebu City Jeepney Commuter: is any person who uses jeepneys within Cebu City for regular or occasional travel





































































CHAPTER II:  REVIEW OF RELATED LITERATURE AND STUDIES

This chapter reviews literature and studies relevant to the architectural design and functional deployment of Pasahero. It establishes a critical academic foundation by examining existing multimodal transit information systems, the digitization of informal public transportation networks such as public utility jeepneys, and the role of Passenger Information Systems (PIS) in mitigating commuter anxiety. By analyzing these existing localized frameworks and algorithmic routing paradigms, this review justifies the development of PasaHero’s specialized "Transportation Mode" and provides a technical rationale for its AI-driven route mapping, land-marked step-by-step guidance, and real-time fare estimation matrix within the unique transit landscape of Cebu City. 





Related Literature

This chapter presents literature relevant to the development of PasaHERO: Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City. It reviews five themes: public transportation in developing countries and the Philippines; route navigation and mapping systems; fare computation technologies; artificial intelligence in transportation; and the gaps that justify PasaHERO. The approach and analysis are underpinned by established theories in transportation and technology adoption, including the Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT), Mobility as a Service (MaaS) frameworks, and the Sustainable Urban Mobility model. These frameworks guide the understanding of commuter behavior, technology integration, and urban transport improvements, establishing the theoretical and practical relevance of PasaHERO for Cebu City commuters.

Public transportation systems in developing countries often face limited digital infrastructure, fragmented services, and a lack of real-time information. Nirwana (2025) found that IoT, GPS, Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), and AI can improve transportation efficiency, but implementation is hindered by infrastructure and interoperability challenges. However, Nirwana’s study focuses mainly on macro-level technological obstacles and offers less insight into commuter-level adoption challenges and the specific needs of informal transport modes such as jeepneys. Yan (2026) similarly reported disconnected ticketing systems and inadequate real-time travel information, but did not address how these issues affect daily commuter decision-making or last-mile connectivity. In the Philippines, jeepneys remain the dominant mode of transportation despite limited digitization and poor access to route and fare information (Reyes et al., 2023; Nesnia, 2026). Existing research on jeepneys tends to emphasize infrastructural shortcomings but often lacks data on user experiences, technology adoption barriers, and practical digitization strategies for jeepney-centric systems.

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) and digital mapping technologies have become essential tools for improving commuter accessibility and route planning. Basnayake (2013) showed that GIS-based transit systems can provide route options, travel time estimates, and fare information. Krambeck (2015) emphasized that the lack of comprehensive transportation data hinders commuter decision-making and transport planning in the Philippine context. More recent studies integrated GIS with real-time tracking technologies, achieving high location accuracy and rapid notification delivery for passengers (Triyuliarsha et al., 2026). These findings justify including jeepney route visualization and digital mapping features in PasaHERO.

Fare transparency remains a major concern in informal transportation systems. Romanuke (2017) showed that GPS-based distance tracking can support automated and transparent fare computation. Assadifard (2014) likewise demonstrated that route-based distance calculations can determine accurate transportation fares. Recent automatic fare collection systems further show that QR codes, NFC, and RFID can enable faster, more transparent, and user-friendly fare management (Anand et al., 2025; Sonawane et al., 2025; Bhavani et al., 2025). These studies support the design of PasaHERO’s fare analytics component, which aims to provide clear, distance-based fare computation for traditional and modernized jeepneys.

Artificial intelligence has increasingly been applied to transportation systems to improve efficiency, personalization, and commuter experience. Koppala et al. (2025) demonstrated AI-driven route matching to optimize transportation services, while Suksomkit et al. (2025) showed that intelligent transit platforms can enhance transparency and user trust. AI applications have also expanded into route optimization and hazard-aware navigation. Betchayda et al. (2025) developed a flood-aware navigation system that reroutes users based on environmental conditions, while Daba-ay et al. (2025) implemented an AI-powered passenger occupancy monitoring system for modern jeepneys in the Philippines. These studies highlight the growing role of AI in smarter transportation systems and support the use of AI in PasaHERO’s route recommendations and commuter alerts.

Despite significant advancements, existing transportation applications still exhibit important limitations. Many systems focus solely on route tracking, fare management, or vehicle monitoring without integrating these features into a single commuter-centered platform. Applications such as iBiyahe and JeePoint provide route information and GPS tracking but lack comprehensive fare analytics, multimodal navigation, and AI-powered recommendations tailored to commuter needs (Fraginal et al., 2024; Pocaan et al., 2025). Specifically, iBiyahe’s platform offers mainly static jeepney route data and basic real-time location updates, while JeePoint focuses on driver and vehicle information and does not support automated fare computation or multimodal trip planning. In contrast, PasaHERO integrates jeepney route visualization, dynamic fare analytics, and AI-driven multimodal navigation into a single solution, offering personalized recommendations and an improved user experience for daily commuters. Cebu City’s jeepney system also remains underrepresented in existing research and technology solutions, which shows why PasaHERO is both novel and necessary.

Overall, the reviewed studies provide strong support for the development of PasaHERO. Research consistently shows that commuters experience difficulties due to insufficient route information, unclear fare structures, and the lack of integrated digital transportation platforms (Nirwana, 2025; Yan, 2026; Nesnia, 2026). In Cebu City, unique commuter challenges further underscore the need for a new approach. The city’s jeepney network is extensive but often lacks digitized route information, leading to confusion for both locals and visitors. Frequent route adjustments, inconsistent vehicle availability, traffic congestion, and limited integration with other transit modes contribute to daily uncertainty for commuters. Fare calculation is also complicated by variable pricing based on distance and periodic regulatory changes, leading to unclear or disputed fares. GIS-based navigation systems, fare computation technologies, and AI-powered transportation applications have demonstrated their effectiveness in improving commuter experience and operational efficiency. PasaHERO addresses this gap by combining multimodal AI navigation, real-time jeepney route visualization, and fare analytics into a single platform specifically designed for Cebu City commuters, offering a more accessible, transparent, and intelligent public transportation experience.







Theoretical Foundation

The development of PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City is based on established theories and frameworks in transportation, information systems, and geographic technologies. These theories provide the conceptual basis for the system's navigation, route visualization, fare computation, and user acceptance. 

Graph Theory

	Graph Theory, established by Euler in 1736 and further developed in modern mathematics, is the study of graphs consisting of vertices (nodes) and edges (connections). In transportation systems, Graph Theory is commonly used to represent road networks, transit stops, and travel routes. Each destination or jeepney stop can be represented as a node, while the roads or jeepney routes connecting them are represented as edges. This representation allows navigation systems to organize transportation networks and determine possible travel paths between locations.

PasaHERO mainly bases on Graph Theory in organizing the jeepney transportation network of Cebu City. Jeepney stops, landmarks and destinations are represented as interconnected locations that enable the system to suggest routes, visualize jeepney paths on a digital map, and offer first-mile and last-mile navigation guidance.

Geographic Information System (GIS) 

Geographic Information System (GIS) is a computer system that collects, manages, analyzes and visualizes geographical data. GIS combines spatial data, such as maps, coordinates, roads and landmarks, to aid in location-based decision-making. It has become an essential technology in the field of transportation planning because it allows the users to visualize the routes, determine locations and improve the accuracy of navigation.

PasaHERO uses GIS concepts such as digital maps, GPS location services and route visualization to help commuters traverse Cebu City’s jeepney network. The system displays color-coded jeepney routes, walking paths, boarding locations, drop-off points and nearby landmarks, enabling commuters to better understand their travel route and destination.



Rule Based System

A Rule-Based System is an AI method that relies on pre-defined rules or conditions to make decisions. The system uses logical “if-then” rules to produce consistent outputs instead of learning from data. Rule-based systems are widely used in transportation for fare computation, route classification and decision support.

PasaHERO utilizes rule-based computation of jeepney fares. The system computes estimated fares based on the official LTFRB distance-based fare matrix for traditional and modernized jeepneys. Commuters can get a transparent and consistent estimation of their fare before starting their journey, as fare rules are predefined.

Mobility as a Service (MaaS) 

Mobility as a Service (MaaS) is a transportation concept that aims to provide seamless travel planning by integrating multiple transportation services into one digital platform. MaaS integrates walking, public transportation and other mobility options into one seamless journey, rather than treating each mode of transportation separately. This framework provides efficient, convenient and user-centric mobility.

PasaHERO’s multimodal navigation capability reflects the principles of MaaS. The app integrates walking directions for first-mile and last-mile travel with jeepney route recommendations, enabling commuters to plan end-to-end journeys from their current location to their final destination in one platform.

Unified Theory of Technology Acceptance and Use (UTAUT)

The Unified Theory of Acceptance and Use of Technology (UTAUT) developed by Venkatesh et al. (2003) explains the determinants of user acceptance and the intention to use new technologies. The model identifies four main determinants of technology adoption: performance expectancy, effort expectancy, social influence and facilitating conditions.

This theory is the basis for the evaluation stage of PasaHERO. The study assesses the functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency and user satisfaction of the system to determine the level of acceptability among commuters. These evaluation criteria are in line with the principles of UTAUT, emphasizing the importance of perceived usefulness and ease of use in technology adoption.



Related Studies

The researchers examined various studies on AI-driven navigation systems, multimodal public transportation routing algorithms, digital map-based jeepney route visualization, and fare calculation methodologies to evaluate and compare the breakthroughs of PasaHERO.  The studies, derived from academic literature and transport planning resources, demonstrate how automated mobile technologies can alleviate transit anxiety, overcome severe information asymmetries in public transport, and enhance metropolitan mobility for newcomers and socially anxious travelers. Through an extensive analysis of related works, the researchers hope to uncover effective navigation strategies and critical digital gaps in localized public transport mapping, which PasaHERO aims to address through its integrated color-coded route overlays, dynamic fare analytics, and conversational AI guidance.

JTransit is a Metro Cebu oriented Philippine native mobile application acts as a navigation app for users in the Philippines based commuters, which originated/made in Cebu. It provides the best jeepneys to take based on where the user is coming from and going, thus making commuting around Metro Cebu more efficient and less burdensome.



Figure 4: Cebu Jeepney Finder

Cebu Jeepney Finder is a web-based commuter guide that assists users find direct jeepney routes and transfers around Cebu. The platform provides access to 55 jeepney routes in Cebu and over 1,100 stops, with recommendations for transfers to make commuting more efficient. It also has the ease of use as it is non-registration, allowing users to easily browse the latest jeepney route information without any barriers. 



Figure 5: RUTA

RUTA is a Cebu-based commuter platform that aims to simplify the alphanumeric jeepney route codes in the city. It decodes codes like 17B, 04B, and 01A into easy-to-read route descriptions so commuters know which jeepneys to ride for their trips. It also suggests routes to help users travel smarter. RUTA is more than just decoding; it also focuses on commuter-friendly explanations and traffic-conscious suggestions, making it a practical tool for navigating Cebu’s busy transport network.

CHAPTER III: DESIGN AND METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The study was designed to gather quantitative data from jeepney commuters in Cebu City to understand their commuting experiences, difficulties, and needs, as well as to facilitate the creation of PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City. The design aimed at collecting quantifiable data on respondents, route navigation practices, fare-related issues, and perceptions towards the suggested system features and mechanism.

A systematic survey questionnaire was utilized as the main tool for gathering data. The collected data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to discern user needs that directed the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application. Following the development of the prototype, it was subjected to system testing and user assessment to evaluate its functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and user satisfaction. The results were utilized to assess the acceptability of the suggested system and pinpoint areas for additional enhancement.



Method

	This study utilized a quantitative research method to gather and examine data regarding the commuting experiences, difficulties, and requirements of jeepney passengers in Cebu City. A systematic survey questionnaire acted as the main research tool for collecting quantitative data from the participants. The survey gathered details regarding the respondents' demographic characteristics, their commuting experiences, difficulties faced when using jeepneys, and their views on the suggested mechanisms and features of PasaHERO.

The survey included closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale, allowing respondents to indicate their level of agreement with every statement. The acquired data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to pinpoint common commuting issues and evaluate the perceived effectiveness of PasaHERO's suggested mechanisms and features.

The quantitative research approach was suitable for this study as it allowed the researchers to systematically gather measurable and standardized information from the intended participants. The results provided the foundation for determining user needs and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested PasaHERO web-based commuter assistance system.



Flow of the Study

The project was carried out in several phases, beginning with the requirements stage, where the system requirements were divided into functional and non-functional requirements. 

The analysis phase focused on the data processing flow, UI/UX design, security and privacy, data storage, reporting tools, and user documentation to establish the overall system requirements and functionality. 

These were then translated into the design phase, where data modeling, Figma prototypes, system architecture, security framework, dashboard design, and user manuals were developed to serve as the blueprint for the application. 

The system then underwent a comprehensive testing phase consisting of system testing, which evaluated functionality, reliability, efficiency, security, route recommendation accuracy, and fare calculation accuracy, along with user testing involving students, daily commuters, first-time travelers, and tourists. 

The resulting system was PasaHERO: Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City, developed using a unified JavaScript technology stack consisting of HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, JavaScript (ES6+), Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, HTML5 Geolocation API, Node.js, Express.js, Sequelize ORM, and MySQL.  

Finally, the review phase assessed the system based on user satisfaction, route recommendation accuracy, fare calculation accuracy, navigation effectiveness, system performance, and overall acceptability to determine the quality and effectiveness of the developed application.

Research Environment 

The research was conducted in Cebu City, one of the major urban area in the Philippines, where public utility jeepneys are commonly utilized for transportation. The city features a widespread jeepney system that links residential areas, educational facilities, businesses, government buildings, and various important locations. Even with many jeepney routes available, passengers frequently face challenges in recognizing suitable routes, calculating fares, finding boarding and drop-off locations, and navigating through unknown areas.

The participants of the research were jeepney passengers from Cebu City. The study focus on this environment as it reflects the real context where the proposed PasaHERO system is intended to operate. Conducting the study in Cebu City allowed the researchers to collect relevant information regarding commuters' experiences, difficulties, and needs, which formed the foundation for the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application.



Research Respondents

The participants in this study were intentionally chosen jeepney commuters in Cebu City. They were selected due to their direct experience with the city's jeepney transportation system and their ability to offer relevant insights about their commuting experiences, difficulties with route navigation, fare-related issues, and views on the suggested features and mechanisms of PasaHERO. The participants comprised students, workers, residents, and various individuals who frequently or occasionally travel by jeepney throughout Cebu City.

The participants filled out a structured survey that collected data on their demographic information, commuting experiences, difficulties faced while using jeepneys, and views on the proposed PasaHERO system. The gathered data formed the foundation for recognizing commuter requirements and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter application.

Research Instrument 

The primary research instrument used in this study was a structured, closed-ended questionnaire designed to collect quantitative data from the respondents. The questionnaire consisted of three parts:

Part I: Respondent Profile gathered demographic and commuting-related information, including age, gender, occupation, frequency of jeepney use, familiarity with Cebu City jeepney routes, and the use of existing navigation applications. 

Part II: Current Commuting Experience identified the respondents' commuting experiences and the challenges they encounter while commuting by jeepney in Cebu City, particularly in terms of route navigation, fare information, and boarding and destination guidance. 

Part III: Perceived Usefulness of the Proposed Features measured the respondents' level of agreement regarding the proposed mechanisms and features of PasaHERO, including AI-assisted route recommendation, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, multimodal navigation, landmark-based guidance, and destination alerts. 

The questionnaire utilized closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale to obtain standardized responses from the respondents. Prior to its administration, the questionnaire was submitted to a panel of experts for content validation to ensure its clarity, relevance, and alignment with the objectives of the study. 

The recommendations provided by the validators were incorporated to improve the quality of the research instrument before its distribution. The data gathered from the questionnaire served as the basis for identifying user requirements and supporting the analysis, design, and development of the proposed PasaHERO system.

AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine 

To provide a clearer understanding of how the proposed PasaHERO system generates route recommendations, the researchers designed the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine shown in Figure 7. The engine serves as the decision-support component of the proposed system, responsible for evaluating multiple travel-related factors before recommending the most appropriate jeepney route. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination, after which the system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database. 















































Figure 7: AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine Architecture 

Figure 7 illustrates the architecture of the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine employed in the proposed PasaHERO system. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination. The system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database before evaluating multiple travel-related factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assessed using predefined recommendation criteria and assigned a recommendation score based on its overall suitability for the commuter's trip. Routes that satisfy favorable travel conditions, such as shorter walking distance, lower estimated fare, fewer transfers, greater route availability, shorter travel distance, and better landmark accessibility, receive higher recommendation scores and are prioritized by the system. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked route is presented together with the corresponding fare estimate, walking directions, boarding and drop-off locations, landmark-based guidance, and a geofenced "Para" notification. This recommendation process enables the system to provide context-aware route recommendations that improve the convenience, accessibility, and overall commuting experience of jeepney passengers in Cebu City.

To determine the most appropriate jeepney route for the commuter, the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates several travel-related factors. Table X presents the evaluation criteria used by the system in assessing and ranking feasible routes.

Program Workflow

This section describes the program workflow of the proposed PasaHERO system. It illustrates the sequence of user interactions and automated system processes that work together to provide intelligent public transportation navigation in Cebu City. The workflow demonstrates how user inputs, real-time location data, jeepney route information, landmark records, and fare data are processed by the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine to generate multimodal route recommendations, interactive route visualizations, fare estimates, and navigation assistance. It also illustrates how the application continuously monitors the commuter's location to provide destination notifications ("Para" alerts) upon approaching the designated drop-off point. The PasaHERO program workflow consists of four interconnected workflows: the Overall Program Workflow, User Workflow, System Workflow, and Admin Workflow, each representing the major operational processes that support the functionality and maintenance of the proposed system. 



Figure 9: Detailed Program Workflow

Illustrates the overall operation of the PasaHERO application, beginning with user authentication and ending with trip completion. After logging into the system, commuters provide their current location through GPS or manual input and enter their intended destination. The system retrieves the necessary route, fare, and landmark information before the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates multiple contextual travel factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assigned a recommendation score based on predefined recommendation criteria. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked multimodal route is selected as the recommended route for the commuter.

The selected route is presented through an interactive map displaying jeepney routes, walking paths, boarding and drop-off points, nearby landmarks, estimated fares, and estimated travel time. During navigation, the application continuously tracks the commuter's real-time location, monitors route progress, and evaluates geofence boundaries to trigger a Destination Notification ("Para" Alert) when the commuter is approaching the designated drop-off location. Once the destination is reached, the system generates a trip summary containing the recommended route taken, estimated fare, travel distance, and travel time, saves the trip history in the database, and allows commuters to optionally provide feedback regarding their travel experience before completing the trip.

 



Figure 10: User Workflow

The User Workflow illustrates how commuters interact with the PasaHERO application throughout their journey. The workflow begins when users access the application and either log in to an existing account or register a new one. After authentication, the system verifies the user's credentials before directing them to the user dashboard. Commuters then set their trip details by providing their current location through GPS auto-detection or manual input and entering their intended destination. The system subsequently generates and displays an AI-powered recommended route that includes the appropriate jeepney route and signboard, first-mile and last-mile walking paths, estimated fare and travel time, boarding and drop-off points, and nearby landmarks. Users then start navigation and receive real-time route guidance, live location updates, and walking directions throughout the trip. As the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point, the application sends a geofenced "PARA" notification to remind the user to prepare for alighting. Once the destination is reached, the system presents a trip summary containing the route taken, estimated fare, distance traveled, and travel time. Users may optionally provide feedback by rating their travel experience before ending their session by logging out of the application. 



Figure 11: System Workflow

The System Workflow describes the internal operations performed by the proposed PasaHERO system after receiving a commuter's travel request. The process begins when the user provides the current location and destination, after which the system validates the input and retrieves the necessary data from the MySQL database, including jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, landmarks and stops, and LTFRB-approved fare rates. The AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine then evaluates all feasible routes by considering multiple contextual travel factors such as walking distance, estimated fare, route availability and frequency, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks and safety. Each route is assigned a recommendation score and ranked according to its overall suitability before the highest-ranked route is selected and presented through an interactive map showing the jeepney route, boarding and drop-off points, walking directions, estimated fare, estimated travel time, and nearby landmarks. During the trip, the system continuously tracks the commuter's location using GPS to provide real-time navigation and automatically sends a geofenced "PARA" notification when the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point. Upon reaching the destination, the system generates a trip summary containing the route taken, fare, travel time, and trip date, allows the commuter to submit feedback, and stores the completed trip in the MySQL database for future reference. 





Figure 12: Admin Workflow

The Admin Workflow illustrates the administrative processes responsible for maintaining the accuracy, reliability, and efficiency of the proposed PasaHERO system. The workflow begins when the administrator securely logs into the admin dashboard using authorized credentials, where system statistics and recent activities are displayed. From the dashboard, the administrator manages jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, LTFRB fare rates, landmarks, jeepney stops, and user accounts to ensure that all navigation data remains accurate and up to date. The administrator also monitors reports and analytics, reviews trip records and statistics, manages announcements and notifications, configures system settings and user permissions, and performs database backup, restoration, maintenance, and integrity checks to preserve data reliability. All administrative updates are securely stored in the MySQL database, allowing the latest information to be reflected throughout the application and ensuring commuters receive accurate route recommendations, fare information, and real-time navigation services before the administrator securely logs out of the system.

Figure 14: Functional decomposition diagram

The Database Management module securely stores user information, jeepney routes, fare data, landmark records, and trip history to support the reliable operation of the proposed system.

Database Design

Entity-Relationship Diagram

An Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) is a graphical representation that illustrates the relationships among the different entities and their corresponding interactions within a database system. For this study, the ERD serves as a blueprint for designing the database of the PasaHERO system by identifying the essential data entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them. It enables the researchers to organize data efficiently, maintain data integrity, and ensure that the system supports the required functionalities such as AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare computation, landmark-based guidance, and commuter notifications. The ERD also provides a structured foundation for database implementation, allowing the application to operate accurately, efficiently, and reliably. 





Figure 28: Entity Relationship Diagram

          Figure 28 illustrates the database structure of the proposed PasaHERO system. The database is centered on the USERS, TRIPS, and JEEPNEY_ROUTES entities, which collectively support commuter registration, trip planning, and intelligent route recommendation. Each user can create multiple trips, while each trip is associated with a selected jeepney route and is composed of one or more TRIP_SEGMENTS that represent the multimodal journey, including walking and jeepney travel. The JEEPNEY_ROUTES entity is linked to STOPS through the ROUTE_STOPS table, allowing the system to maintain the sequence of boarding and drop-off locations. Nearby LANDMARKS are associated with stops to provide landmark-based navigation and commuter guidance. The FARE_MATRIX entity stores the LTFRB-approved distance-based fare information used to estimate transportation costs, while the NOTIFICATIONS entity manages destination notifications ("Para" alerts) and other trip-related updates. The FEEDBACK entity stores commuter ratings and comments after each completed trip. Collectively, these entities establish a normalized relational database implemented in MySQL, providing reliable data management for user information, route visualization, fare computation, trip history, notifications, and the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine that powers the PasaHERO system.

Technology Stack 

	Pasahero consists of a web-based commuter assistance platform designed to help users navigate public transportation routes within Cebu City through AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, and real-time commuter guidance. To ensure efficient system performance, responsive user interaction, and reliable processing of transportation data, the development process utilizes a modern technology stack that supports scalable web application development, dynamic user interfaces, and secure database management. 





Figure 30: Server Technology Stack

            		Figure XX illustrates the technology stack utilized in the development of PasaHERO, which adopts a full-stack web architecture composed of frontend, backend, database, mapping, intelligent route processing, and deployment technologies. The frontend is developed using HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript (ES6+) to provide a responsive and user-friendly interface. The backend is powered by Node.js and Express.js, which handle server-side processing, RESTful API development, authentication, and communication between the application and the database. MySQL serves as the relational database management system, while Sequelize ORM simplifies database interactions and manages relationships among users, jeepney routes, stops, landmarks, fare matrices, trips, notifications, and reports. For mapping and navigation, the system integrates Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, and the HTML5 Geolocation API to support interactive route visualization, first-mile and last-mile guidance, real-time location tracking, and geofenced destination alerts. PasaHERO also incorporates a rule-based route recommendation engine and LTFRB distance-based fare engine to generate route recommendations and fare estimates based on predefined transportation data and fare rules. The system is deployed through cloud hosting with CDN and load-balancing support to ensure accessibility, reliability, and performance.
