These questions focus on justification, critical thinking, AI, research methodology, and common panel follow-ups.

Question 101

Q: Why did you choose these theories instead of others?

Best Answer:

We selected Graph Theory, GIS, and Decision Support System because they directly support the transportation network, spatial visualization, and intelligent route recommendation implemented in PasaHERO.

Reviewer Tip: Always connect the theory to a system feature.

Question 102

Q: Why not use Dijkstra's Algorithm?

Best Answer:

Dijkstra computes the shortest path based mainly on distance. Our system evaluates multiple travel criteria such as fare, transfers, walking distance, route availability, and landmarks.

Reviewer Tip: Don't say Dijkstra is wrong.

Question 103

Q: Can Dijkstra be integrated in the future?

Best Answer:

Yes. It can be used as a shortest-path component while the DSS evaluates multiple recommendation criteria.

Question 104

Q: Why is your recommendation engine better than shortest path?

Best Answer:

Because commuters consider more than distance. They also consider cost, convenience, transfers, and accessibility.

Question 105

Q: Isn't your system just a rule-based algorithm?

Best Answer:

It is a rule-based intelligent Decision Support System that evaluates multiple criteria to assist commuter decision-making.

Reviewer Tip: Acknowledge the implementation honestly.

Question 106

Q: Why do you still call it AI?
