PasaHERO Defense BibleVersion 2 - Reviewer Part 1(Questions 1-50)

How to Use This Reviewer

Each question includes: the likely panel question, the best answer, and a reviewer tip. Focus on understanding the reasoning rather than memorizing exact words.

Question 1

Q: Please introduce your study.

Best Answer:

Our study is entitled 'PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City.' It is a mobile navigation system designed to help commuters by providing route recommendations, fare estimation, landmark guidance, and destination notifications.

Reviewer Tip: Start with confidence and mention the title exactly.

Question 2

Q: Why did you choose this topic?

Best Answer:

We chose this topic because many commuters experience difficulty identifying the correct jeepney, fare, transfer points, and destination. PasaHERO addresses these real transportation problems.

Reviewer Tip: Relate the answer to commuters.

Question 3

Q: Why Cebu City?

Best Answer:

The study focuses on Cebu City because the route database, landmarks, and fare matrix are based on Cebu City's jeepney transportation system.

Reviewer Tip: Mention scope.

Question 4

Q: Who are the beneficiaries?

Best Answer:

Commuters, students, tourists, researchers, and local government units benefit from improved navigation and transportation planning.

Reviewer Tip: Know every beneficiary.

Question 5

Q: What makes PasaHERO unique?

Best Answer:

It integrates AI-assisted route recommendation, fare estimation, route visualization, landmark guidance, walking navigation, and Para Alert in one platform.

Reviewer Tip: Avoid saying 'because no one has done it'.

Question 6

Q: Why jeepneys only?

Best Answer:

The scope is limited to traditional jeepneys to keep the study manageable and focused.

Reviewer Tip: Mention future enhancements.

Question 7

Q: What is multimodal navigation?

Best Answer:

It combines walking and jeepney travel within one navigation experience.

Reviewer Tip: Simple definition.

Question 8

Q: Why a mobile application?

Best Answer:

Smartphones are widely used and provide GPS, maps, and notifications needed by the system.

Reviewer Tip: Mention convenience.

Question 9

Q: Why is this research?

Best Answer:

The system was developed after identifying a real problem, reviewing literature, conducting surveys, and applying established theories.

Reviewer Tip: Research first, software second.

Question 10

Q: Explain the research gap.

Best Answer:

Existing applications lack an integrated Cebu-specific jeepney navigation solution combining recommendation, fare analytics, and landmark guidance.

Reviewer Tip: Mention comparison.

Question 11

Q: Why Graph Theory?

Best Answer:

Graph Theory models transportation networks using nodes and edges, making it suitable for jeepney routes.

Reviewer Tip: Connect to system.

Question 12

Q: Where is Graph Theory applied?

Best Answer:

It organizes stops, landmarks, and routes before route evaluation.

Reviewer Tip: Don't confuse with DSS.

Question 13

Q: Why GIS?

Best Answer:

GIS provides spatial analysis, digital maps, GPS integration, and route visualization.

Reviewer Tip: Know GPS vs GIS.

Question 14

Q: Difference between GPS and GIS?

Best Answer:

GPS determines the user's location, while GIS manages and visualizes geographic information.

Reviewer Tip: Very common.

Question 15

Q: Why Decision Support System?

Best Answer:

The system evaluates multiple travel criteria and recommends the best route using weighted decision rules.

Reviewer Tip: Key answer.

Question 16

Q: Why not Machine Learning?

Best Answer:

The system does not require historical training data. It evaluates predefined criteria, making DSS more suitable.

Reviewer Tip: Be honest.

Question 17

Q: Where is the AI?

Best Answer:

The AI is implemented through an intelligent Decision Support System that ranks feasible routes using weighted criteria.

Reviewer Tip: Do not claim ML.

Question 18

Q: Why is this AI and not just an algorithm?

Best Answer:

It performs intelligent multi-criteria decision-making rather than a single calculation. The intelligence comes from evaluating several contextual factors before making a recommendation.

Reviewer Tip: Most important.

Question 19

Q: Explain your recommendation engine.

Best Answer:

It retrieves route data, evaluates multiple criteria, computes recommendation scores, ranks feasible routes, and recommends the highest-ranked option.

Reviewer Tip: Know the workflow.

Question 20

Q: Why these recommendation weights?

Best Answer:

They were assigned based on literature review and the relative importance of each travel factor.

Reviewer Tip: Mention justification.

Question 21

Q: Why walking distance has higher weight?

It directly affects commuter convenience and accessibility.

Q: Why nearby landmarks?
