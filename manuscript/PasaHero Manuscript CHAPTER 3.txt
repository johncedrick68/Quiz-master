CHAPTER III: DESIGN AND METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The study was designed to gather quantitative data from jeepney commuters in Cebu City to understand their commuting experiences, difficulties, and needs, as well as to facilitate the creation of PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City. The design aimed at collecting quantifiable data on respondents, route navigation practices, fare-related issues, and perceptions towards the suggested system features and mechanism.

A systematic survey questionnaire was utilized as the main tool for gathering data. The collected data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to discern user needs that directed the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application. Following the development of the prototype, it was subjected to system testing and user assessment to evaluate its functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and user satisfaction. The results were utilized to assess the acceptability of the suggested system and pinpoint areas for additional enhancement.



Method

	This study utilized a quantitative research method to gather and examine data regarding the commuting experiences, difficulties, and requirements of jeepney passengers in Cebu City. A systematic survey questionnaire acted as the main research tool for collecting quantitative data from the participants. The survey gathered details regarding the respondents' demographic characteristics, their commuting experiences, difficulties faced when using jeepneys, and their views on the suggested mechanisms and features of PasaHERO.

The survey included closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale, allowing respondents to indicate their level of agreement with every statement. The acquired data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to pinpoint common commuting issues and evaluate the perceived effectiveness of PasaHERO's suggested mechanisms and features.

The quantitative research approach was suitable for this study as it allowed the researchers to systematically gather measurable and standardized information from the intended participants. The results provided the foundation for determining user needs and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested PasaHERO web-based commuter assistance system.



Flow of the Study

The project was carried out in several phases, beginning with the requirements stage, where the system requirements were divided into functional and non-functional requirements. 

The analysis phase focused on the data processing flow, UI/UX design, security and privacy, data storage, reporting tools, and user documentation to establish the overall system requirements and functionality. 

These were then translated into the design phase, where data modeling, Figma prototypes, system architecture, security framework, dashboard design, and user manuals were developed to serve as the blueprint for the application. 

The system then underwent a comprehensive testing phase consisting of system testing, which evaluated functionality, reliability, efficiency, security, route recommendation accuracy, and fare calculation accuracy, along with user testing involving students, daily commuters, first-time travelers, and tourists. 

The resulting system was PasaHERO: Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City, developed using a unified JavaScript technology stack consisting of HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, JavaScript (ES6+), Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, HTML5 Geolocation API, Node.js, Express.js, Sequelize ORM, and MySQL.  

Finally, the review phase assessed the system based on user satisfaction, route recommendation accuracy, fare calculation accuracy, navigation effectiveness, system performance, and overall acceptability to determine the quality and effectiveness of the developed application.

Research Environment 

The research was conducted in Cebu City, one of the major urban area in the Philippines, where public utility jeepneys are commonly utilized for transportation. The city features a widespread jeepney system that links residential areas, educational facilities, businesses, government buildings, and various important locations. Even with many jeepney routes available, passengers frequently face challenges in recognizing suitable routes, calculating fares, finding boarding and drop-off locations, and navigating through unknown areas.

The participants of the research were jeepney passengers from Cebu City. The study focus on this environment as it reflects the real context where the proposed PasaHERO system is intended to operate. Conducting the study in Cebu City allowed the researchers to collect relevant information regarding commuters' experiences, difficulties, and needs, which formed the foundation for the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application.



Research Respondents

The participants in this study were intentionally chosen jeepney commuters in Cebu City. They were selected due to their direct experience with the city's jeepney transportation system and their ability to offer relevant insights about their commuting experiences, difficulties with route navigation, fare-related issues, and views on the suggested features and mechanisms of PasaHERO. The participants comprised students, workers, residents, and various individuals who frequently or occasionally travel by jeepney throughout Cebu City.

The participants filled out a structured survey that collected data on their demographic information, commuting experiences, difficulties faced while using jeepneys, and views on the proposed PasaHERO system. The gathered data formed the foundation for recognizing commuter requirements and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter application.





Sampling Method

The research utilized purposive sampling to choose the participants. This non-probability sampling method was suitable as it focused on individuals who had direct experience commuting by jeepney in Cebu City. These individuals were viewed as able to offer valuable perspectives on their commuting experiences, difficulties in route navigation, issues regarding fares, and views on the suggested mechanisms and features of PasaHERO.

The participants consisted of students, workers, locals, and others who frequently or occasionally travel by jeepney throughout Cebu City. The information collected from these participants formed the foundation for recognizing commuter requirements and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested PasaHERO system.



Research Instrument 

The primary research instrument used in this study was a structured, closed-ended questionnaire designed to collect quantitative data from the respondents. The questionnaire consisted of three parts:

Part I: Respondent Profile gathered demographic and commuting-related information, including age, gender, occupation, frequency of jeepney use, familiarity with Cebu City jeepney routes, and the use of existing navigation applications. 

Part II: Current Commuting Experience identified the respondents' commuting experiences and the challenges they encounter while commuting by jeepney in Cebu City, particularly in terms of route navigation, fare information, and boarding and destination guidance. 

Part III: Perceived Usefulness of the Proposed Features measured the respondents' level of agreement regarding the proposed mechanisms and features of PasaHERO, including AI-assisted route recommendation, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, multimodal navigation, landmark-based guidance, and destination alerts. 

The questionnaire utilized closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale to obtain standardized responses from the respondents. Prior to its administration, the questionnaire was submitted to a panel of experts for content validation to ensure its clarity, relevance, and alignment with the objectives of the study. 

The recommendations provided by the validators were incorporated to improve the quality of the research instrument before its distribution. The data gathered from the questionnaire served as the basis for identifying user requirements and supporting the analysis, design, and development of the proposed PasaHERO system.

The research procedure was carried out through a series of systematic phases. The study began by identifying the transportation-related problems encountered by jeepney commuters in Cebu City and establishing the objectives of the research. Afterward, a structured survey questionnaire was developed and administered to selected jeepney commuters to gather quantitative data regarding their demographic profile, commuting experiences, challenges, and perceptions of the proposed mechanisms and features of the system. Following the development phase, the prototype underwent system testing to verify that its modules and core functionalities operated according to the specified requirements. The findings from the testing process were analyzed to identify necessary refinements and improvements prior to the implementation of subsequent system evaluation activities.

AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine 

To provide a clearer understanding of how the proposed PasaHERO system generates route recommendations, the researchers designed the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine shown in Figure 7. The engine serves as the decision-support component of the proposed system, responsible for evaluating multiple travel-related factors before recommending the most appropriate jeepney route. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination, after which the system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database. 















































Figure 7: AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine Architecture 

Figure 7 illustrates the architecture of the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine employed in the proposed PasaHERO system. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination. The system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database before evaluating multiple travel-related factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assessed using predefined recommendation criteria and assigned a recommendation score based on its overall suitability for the commuter's trip. Routes that satisfy favorable travel conditions, such as shorter walking distance, lower estimated fare, fewer transfers, greater route availability, shorter travel distance, and better landmark accessibility, receive higher recommendation scores and are prioritized by the system. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked route is presented together with the corresponding fare estimate, walking directions, boarding and drop-off locations, landmark-based guidance, and a geofenced "Para" notification. This recommendation process enables the system to provide context-aware route recommendations that improve the convenience, accessibility, and overall commuting experience of jeepney passengers in Cebu City.





To determine the most appropriate jeepney route for the commuter, the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates several travel-related factors. Table X presents the evaluation criteria used by the system in assessing and ranking feasible routes.

Program Workflow

This section describes the program workflow of the proposed PasaHERO system. It illustrates the sequence of user interactions and automated system processes that work together to provide intelligent public transportation navigation in Cebu City. The workflow demonstrates how user inputs, real-time location data, jeepney route information, landmark records, and fare data are processed by the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine to generate multimodal route recommendations, interactive route visualizations, fare estimates, and navigation assistance. It also illustrates how the application continuously monitors the commuter's location to provide destination notifications ("Para" alerts) upon approaching the designated drop-off point. The PasaHERO program workflow consists of four interconnected workflows: the Overall Program Workflow, User Workflow, System Workflow, and Admin Workflow, each representing the major operational processes that support the functionality and maintenance of the proposed system. 



Figure 9: Detailed Program Workflow

Illustrates the overall operation of the PasaHERO application, beginning with user authentication and ending with trip completion. After logging into the system, commuters provide their current location through GPS or manual input and enter their intended destination. The system retrieves the necessary route, fare, and landmark information before the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates multiple contextual travel factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assigned a recommendation score based on predefined recommendation criteria. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked multimodal route is selected as the recommended route for the commuter.

The selected route is presented through an interactive map displaying jeepney routes, walking paths, boarding and drop-off points, nearby landmarks, estimated fares, and estimated travel time. During navigation, the application continuously tracks the commuter's real-time location, monitors route progress, and evaluates geofence boundaries to trigger a Destination Notification ("Para" Alert) when the commuter is approaching the designated drop-off location. Once the destination is reached, the system generates a trip summary containing the recommended route taken, estimated fare, travel distance, and travel time, saves the trip history in the database, and allows commuters to optionally provide feedback regarding their travel experience before completing the trip.

 



Figure 10: User Workflow

The User Workflow illustrates how commuters interact with the PasaHERO application throughout their journey. The workflow begins when users access the application and either log in to an existing account or register a new one. After authentication, the system verifies the user's credentials before directing them to the user dashboard. Commuters then set their trip details by providing their current location through GPS auto-detection or manual input and entering their intended destination. The system subsequently generates and displays an AI-powered recommended route that includes the appropriate jeepney route and signboard, first-mile and last-mile walking paths, estimated fare and travel time, boarding and drop-off points, and nearby landmarks. Users then start navigation and receive real-time route guidance, live location updates, and walking directions throughout the trip. As the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point, the application sends a geofenced "PARA" notification to remind the user to prepare for alighting. Once the destination is reached, the system presents a trip summary containing the route taken, estimated fare, distance traveled, and travel time. Users may optionally provide feedback by rating their travel experience before ending their session by logging out of the application. 



Figure 11: System Workflow

The System Workflow describes the internal operations performed by the proposed PasaHERO system after receiving a commuter's travel request. The process begins when the user provides the current location and destination, after which the system validates the input and retrieves the necessary data from the MySQL database, including jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, landmarks and stops, and LTFRB-approved fare rates. The AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine then evaluates all feasible routes by considering multiple contextual travel factors such as walking distance, estimated fare, route availability and frequency, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks and safety. Each route is assigned a recommendation score and ranked according to its overall suitability before the highest-ranked route is selected and presented through an interactive map showing the jeepney route, boarding and drop-off points, walking directions, estimated fare, estimated travel time, and nearby landmarks. During the trip, the system continuously tracks the commuter's location using GPS to provide real-time navigation and automatically sends a geofenced "PARA" notification when the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point. Upon reaching the destination, the system generates a trip summary containing the route taken, fare, travel time, and trip date, allows the commuter to submit feedback, and stores the completed trip in the MySQL database for future reference. 





Figure 12: Admin Workflow

The Admin Workflow illustrates the administrative processes responsible for maintaining the accuracy, reliability, and efficiency of the proposed PasaHERO system. The workflow begins when the administrator securely logs into the admin dashboard using authorized credentials, where system statistics and recent activities are displayed. From the dashboard, the administrator manages jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, LTFRB fare rates, landmarks, jeepney stops, and user accounts to ensure that all navigation data remains accurate and up to date. The administrator also monitors reports and analytics, reviews trip records and statistics, manages announcements and notifications, configures system settings and user permissions, and performs database backup, restoration, maintenance, and integrity checks to preserve data reliability. All administrative updates are securely stored in the MySQL database, allowing the latest information to be reflected throughout the application and ensuring commuters receive accurate route recommendations, fare information, and real-time navigation services before the administrator securely logs out of the system.

Database Design

Entity-Relationship Diagram

An Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) is a graphical representation that illustrates the relationships among the different entities and their corresponding interactions within a database system. For this study, the ERD serves as a blueprint for designing the database of the PasaHERO system by identifying the essential data entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them. It enables the researchers to organize data efficiently, maintain data integrity, and ensure that the system supports the required functionalities such as AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare computation, landmark-based guidance, and commuter notifications. The ERD also provides a structured foundation for database implementation, allowing the application to operate accurately, efficiently, and reliably. 





Figure 28: Entity Relationship Diagram

          Figure 28 illustrates the database structure of the proposed PasaHERO system. The database is centered on the USERS, TRIPS, and JEEPNEY_ROUTES entities, which collectively support commuter registration, trip planning, and intelligent route recommendation. Each user can create multiple trips, while each trip is associated with a selected jeepney route and is composed of one or more TRIP_SEGMENTS that represent the multimodal journey, including walking and jeepney travel. The JEEPNEY_ROUTES entity is linked to STOPS through the ROUTE_STOPS table, allowing the system to maintain the sequence of boarding and drop-off locations. Nearby LANDMARKS are associated with stops to provide landmark-based navigation and commuter guidance. The FARE_MATRIX entity stores the LTFRB-approved distance-based fare information used to estimate transportation costs, while the NOTIFICATIONS entity manages destination notifications ("Para" alerts) and other trip-related updates. The FEEDBACK entity stores commuter ratings and comments after each completed trip. Collectively, these entities establish a normalized relational database implemented in MySQL, providing reliable data management for user information, route visualization, fare computation, trip history, notifications, and the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine that powers the PasaHERO system.

Technology Stack 

	PasaHERO consists of a web-based commuter assistance platform designed to help users navigate public transportation routes within Cebu City through AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, and real-time commuter guidance. To ensure efficient system performance, responsive user interaction, and reliable processing of transportation data, the development process utilizes a modern technology stack that supports scalable web application development, dynamic user interfaces, and secure database management. 





Figure 30: Server Technology Stack

            		Figure XX illustrates the technology stack utilized in the development of PasaHERO, which adopts a full-stack web architecture composed of frontend, backend, database, mapping, intelligent route processing, and deployment technologies. The frontend is developed using HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript (ES6+) to provide a responsive and user-friendly interface. The backend is powered by Node.js and Express.js, which handle server-side processing, RESTful API development, authentication, and communication between the application and the database. MySQL serves as the relational database management system, while Sequelize ORM simplifies database interactions and manages relationships among users, jeepney routes, stops, landmarks, fare matrices, trips, notifications, and reports. For mapping and navigation, the system integrates Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, and the HTML5 Geolocation API to support interactive route visualization, first-mile and last-mile guidance, real-time location tracking, and geofenced destination alerts. PasaHERO also incorporates a AI-Based Rule-Based Route Recommendation Engine and LTFRB distance-based fare engine to generate route recommendations and fare estimates based on predefined transportation data and fare rules. The system is deployed through cloud hosting with CDN and load-balancing support to ensure accessibility, reliability, and performance.

CHAPTER III: DESIGN AND METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The study was designed to gather quantitative data from jeepney commuters in Cebu City to understand their commuting experiences, difficulties, and needs, as well as to facilitate the creation of PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City. The design aimed at collecting quantifiable data on respondents, route navigation practices, fare-related issues, and perceptions towards the suggested system features and mechanism.

A systematic survey questionnaire was utilized as the main tool for gathering data. The collected data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to discern user needs that directed the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application. Following the development of the prototype, it was subjected to system testing and user assessment to evaluate its functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and user satisfaction. The results were utilized to assess the acceptability of the suggested system and pinpoint areas for additional enhancement.



Method

	This study utilized a quantitative research method to gather and examine data regarding the commuting experiences, difficulties, and requirements of jeepney passengers in Cebu City. A systematic survey questionnaire acted as the main research tool for collecting quantitative data from the participants. The survey gathered details regarding the respondents' demographic characteristics, their commuting experiences, difficulties faced when using jeepneys, and their views on the suggested mechanisms and features of PasaHERO.

The survey included closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale, allowing respondents to indicate their level of agreement with every statement. The acquired data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to pinpoint common commuting issues and evaluate the perceived effectiveness of PasaHERO's suggested mechanisms and features.

The quantitative research approach was suitable for this study as it allowed the researchers to systematically gather measurable and standardized information from the intended participants. The results provided the foundation for determining user needs and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested PasaHERO web-based commuter assistance system.



Flow of the Study

The project was carried out in several phases, beginning with the requirements stage, where the system requirements were divided into functional and non-functional requirements. 

The analysis phase focused on the data processing flow, UI/UX design, security and privacy, data storage, reporting tools, and user documentation to establish the overall system requirements and functionality. 

These were then translated into the design phase, where data modeling, Figma prototypes, system architecture, security framework, dashboard design, and user manuals were developed to serve as the blueprint for the application. 

The system then underwent a comprehensive testing phase consisting of system testing, which evaluated functionality, reliability, efficiency, security, route recommendation accuracy, and fare calculation accuracy, along with user testing involving students, daily commuters, first-time travelers, and tourists. 

The resulting system was PasaHERO: Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City, developed using a unified JavaScript technology stack consisting of HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, JavaScript (ES6+), Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, HTML5 Geolocation API, Node.js, Express.js, Sequelize ORM, and MySQL.  

Finally, the review phase assessed the system based on user satisfaction, route recommendation accuracy, fare calculation accuracy, navigation effectiveness, system performance, and overall acceptability to determine the quality and effectiveness of the developed application.

Research Environment 

The research was conducted in Cebu City, one of the major urban area in the Philippines, where public utility jeepneys are commonly utilized for transportation. The city features a widespread jeepney system that links residential areas, educational facilities, businesses, government buildings, and various important locations. Even with many jeepney routes available, passengers frequently face challenges in recognizing suitable routes, calculating fares, finding boarding and drop-off locations, and navigating through unknown areas.

The participants of the research were jeepney passengers from Cebu City. The study focus on this environment as it reflects the real context where the proposed PasaHERO system is intended to operate. Conducting the study in Cebu City allowed the researchers to collect relevant information regarding commuters' experiences, difficulties, and needs, which formed the foundation for the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application.

The participants in this study were intentionally chosen jeepney commuters in Cebu City. Part III: Perceived Usefulness of the Proposed Features measured the respondents' level of agreement regarding the proposed mechanisms and features of PasaHERO, including AI-assisted route recommendation, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, multimodal navigation, landmark-based guidance, and destination alerts. 

The questionnaire utilized closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale to obtain standardized responses from the respondents. The recommendations provided by the validators were incorporated to improve the quality of the research instrument before its distribution. AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine 

To provide a clearer understanding of how the proposed PasaHERO system generates route recommendations, the researchers designed the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine shown in Figure 7. The engine serves as the decision-support component of the proposed system, responsible for evaluating multiple travel-related factors before recommending the most appropriate jeepney route. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination, after which the system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database. 















































Figure 7: AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine Architecture 

Figure 7 illustrates the architecture of the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine employed in the proposed PasaHERO system. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination. The system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database before evaluating multiple travel-related factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assessed using predefined recommendation criteria and assigned a recommendation score based on its overall suitability for the commuter's trip. Routes that satisfy favorable travel conditions, such as shorter walking distance, lower estimated fare, fewer transfers, greater route availability, shorter travel distance, and better landmark accessibility, receive higher recommendation scores and are prioritized by the system. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked route is presented together with the corresponding fare estimate, walking directions, boarding and drop-off locations, landmark-based guidance, and a geofenced "Para" notification. This recommendation process enables the system to provide context-aware route recommendations that improve the convenience, accessibility, and overall commuting experience of jeepney passengers in Cebu City.

Recommendation Score Computation

To determine the most appropriate jeepney route, the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine applies a weighted multi-criteria decision model. Each feasible route is evaluated using predefined recommendation factors, with each factor assigned a corresponding weight based on its relative importance in route selection. The route obtaining the highest overall recommendation score is presented as the recommended route.

The recommendation score is computed using the following equation:

where:

WD = Walking Distance

EF = Estimated Fare

RA = Route Availability

TC = Transfer Count

TD = Travel Distance

LA = Landmark Accessibility

The computed recommendation scores are then compared and ranked. The route with the highest score is selected and recommended to the commuter.

Program Workflow

This section describes the program workflow of the proposed PasaHERO system. It illustrates the sequence of user interactions and automated system processes that work together to provide intelligent public transportation navigation in Cebu City. The workflow demonstrates how user inputs, real-time location data, jeepney route information, landmark records, and fare data are processed by the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine to generate multimodal route recommendations, interactive route visualizations, fare estimates, and navigation assistance. It also illustrates how the application continuously monitors the commuter's location to provide destination notifications ("Para" alerts) upon approaching the designated drop-off point. The PasaHERO program workflow consists of four interconnected workflows: the Overall Program Workflow, User Workflow, System Workflow, and Admin Workflow, each representing the major operational processes that support the functionality and maintenance of the proposed system. 



Figure 9: Detailed Program Workflow

Illustrates the overall operation of the PasaHERO application, beginning with user authentication and ending with trip completion. After logging into the system, commuters provide their current location through GPS or manual input and enter their intended destination. The system retrieves the necessary route, fare, and landmark information before the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates multiple contextual travel factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assigned a recommendation score based on predefined recommendation criteria. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked multimodal route is selected as the recommended route for the commuter.

The selected route is presented through an interactive map displaying jeepney routes, walking paths, boarding and drop-off points, nearby landmarks, estimated fares, and estimated travel time. During navigation, the application continuously tracks the commuter's real-time location, monitors route progress, and evaluates geofence boundaries to trigger a Destination Notification ("Para" Alert) when the commuter is approaching the designated drop-off location. Once the destination is reached, the system generates a trip summary containing the recommended route taken, estimated fare, travel distance, and travel time, saves the trip history in the database, and allows commuters to optionally provide feedback regarding their travel experience before completing the trip.

 



Figure 10: User Workflow

The User Workflow illustrates how commuters interact with the PasaHERO application throughout their journey. The workflow begins when users access the application and either log in to an existing account or register a new one. After authentication, the system verifies the user's credentials before directing them to the user dashboard. Commuters then set their trip details by providing their current location through GPS auto-detection or manual input and entering their intended destination. The system subsequently generates and displays an AI-powered recommended route that includes the appropriate jeepney route and signboard, first-mile and last-mile walking paths, estimated fare and travel time, boarding and drop-off points, and nearby landmarks. Users then start navigation and receive real-time route guidance, live location updates, and walking directions throughout the trip. As the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point, the application sends a geofenced "PARA" notification to remind the user to prepare for alighting. Once the destination is reached, the system presents a trip summary containing the route taken, estimated fare, distance traveled, and travel time. Users may optionally provide feedback by rating their travel experience before ending their session by logging out of the application. 



Figure 11: System Workflow

The System Workflow describes the internal operations performed by the proposed PasaHERO system after receiving a commuter's travel request. The process begins when the user provides the current location and destination, after which the system validates the input and retrieves the necessary data from the MySQL database, including jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, landmarks and stops, and LTFRB-approved fare rates. The AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine then evaluates all feasible routes by considering multiple contextual travel factors such as walking distance, estimated fare, route availability and frequency, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks and safety. Each route is assigned a recommendation score and ranked according to its overall suitability before the highest-ranked route is selected and presented through an interactive map showing the jeepney route, boarding and drop-off points, walking directions, estimated fare, estimated travel time, and nearby landmarks. During the trip, the system continuously tracks the commuter's location using GPS to provide real-time navigation and automatically sends a geofenced "PARA" notification when the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point. Upon reaching the destination, the system generates a trip summary containing the route taken, fare, travel time, and trip date, allows the commuter to submit feedback, and stores the completed trip in the MySQL database for future reference. 





Figure 12: Admin Workflow

The Admin Workflow illustrates the administrative processes responsible for maintaining the accuracy, reliability, and efficiency of the proposed PasaHERO system. The workflow begins when the administrator securely logs into the admin dashboard using authorized credentials, where system statistics and recent activities are displayed. From the dashboard, the administrator manages jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, LTFRB fare rates, landmarks, jeepney stops, and user accounts to ensure that all navigation data remains accurate and up to date. The administrator also monitors reports and analytics, reviews trip records and statistics, manages announcements and notifications, configures system settings and user permissions, and performs database backup, restoration, maintenance, and integrity checks to preserve data reliability. All administrative updates are securely stored in the MySQL database, allowing the latest information to be reflected throughout the application and ensuring commuters receive accurate route recommendations, fare information, and real-time navigation services before the administrator securely logs out of the system.

Database Design

Entity-Relationship Diagram

An Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) is a graphical representation that illustrates the relationships among the different entities and their corresponding interactions within a database system. For this study, the ERD serves as a blueprint for designing the database of the PasaHERO system by identifying the essential data entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them. It enables the researchers to organize data efficiently, maintain data integrity, and ensure that the system supports the required functionalities such as AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare computation, landmark-based guidance, and commuter notifications. The ERD also provides a structured foundation for database implementation, allowing the application to operate accurately, efficiently, and reliably. 





Figure 28: Entity Relationship Diagram

          Figure 28 illustrates the database structure of the proposed PasaHERO system. The database is centered on the USERS, TRIPS, and JEEPNEY_ROUTES entities, which collectively support commuter registration, trip planning, and intelligent route recommendation. Each user can create multiple trips, while each trip is associated with a selected jeepney route and is composed of one or more TRIP_SEGMENTS that represent the multimodal journey, including walking and jeepney travel. The JEEPNEY_ROUTES entity is linked to STOPS through the ROUTE_STOPS table, allowing the system to maintain the sequence of boarding and drop-off locations. Nearby LANDMARKS are associated with stops to provide landmark-based navigation and commuter guidance. The FARE_MATRIX entity stores the LTFRB-approved distance-based fare information used to estimate transportation costs, while the NOTIFICATIONS entity manages destination notifications ("Para" alerts) and other trip-related updates. The FEEDBACK entity stores commuter ratings and comments after each completed trip. Collectively, these entities establish a normalized relational database implemented in MySQL, providing reliable data management for user information, route visualization, fare computation, trip history, notifications, and the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine that powers the PasaHERO system.

Technology Stack 

	PasaHERO consists of a web-based commuter assistance platform designed to help users navigate public transportation routes within Cebu City through AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, and real-time commuter guidance. To ensure efficient system performance, responsive user interaction, and reliable processing of transportation data, the development process utilizes a modern technology stack that supports scalable web application development, dynamic user interfaces, and secure database management. 





Figure 30: Server Technology Stack

            		Figure XX illustrates the technology stack utilized in the development of PasaHERO, which adopts a full-stack web architecture composed of frontend, backend, database, mapping, intelligent route processing, and deployment technologies. The frontend is developed using HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript (ES6+) to provide a responsive and user-friendly interface. The backend is powered by Node.js and Express.js, which handle server-side processing, RESTful API development, authentication, and communication between the application and the database. MySQL serves as the relational database management system, while Sequelize ORM simplifies database interactions and manages relationships among users, jeepney routes, stops, landmarks, fare matrices, trips, notifications, and reports. For mapping and navigation, the system integrates Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, and the HTML5 Geolocation API to support interactive route visualization, first-mile and last-mile guidance, real-time location tracking, and geofenced destination alerts. PasaHERO also incorporates a AI-Based Rule-Based Route Recommendation Engine and LTFRB distance-based fare engine to generate route recommendations and fare estimates based on predefined transportation data and fare rules. The system is deployed through cloud hosting with CDN and load-balancing support to ensure accessibility, reliability, and performance.

CHAPTER III: DESIGN AND METHODOLOGY

Research Design

The study was designed to gather quantitative data from jeepney commuters in Cebu City to understand their commuting experiences, difficulties, and needs, as well as to facilitate the creation of PasaHERO: A Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City. The design aimed at collecting quantifiable data on respondents, route navigation practices, fare-related issues, and perceptions towards the suggested system features and mechanism.

A systematic survey questionnaire was utilized as the main tool for gathering data. The collected data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to discern user needs that directed the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested web-based commuter assistance application. Following the development of the prototype, it was subjected to system testing and user assessment to evaluate its functionality, usability, reliability, efficiency, and user satisfaction. The results were utilized to assess the acceptability of the suggested system and pinpoint areas for additional enhancement.



Method

	This study utilized a quantitative research method to gather and examine data regarding the commuting experiences, difficulties, and requirements of jeepney passengers in Cebu City. A systematic survey questionnaire acted as the main research tool for collecting quantitative data from the participants. The survey gathered details regarding the respondents' demographic characteristics, their commuting experiences, difficulties faced when using jeepneys, and their views on the suggested mechanisms and features of PasaHERO.

The survey included closed-ended questions with a four-point Likert scale, allowing respondents to indicate their level of agreement with every statement. The acquired data were summarized and examined through descriptive statistics to pinpoint common commuting issues and evaluate the perceived effectiveness of PasaHERO's suggested mechanisms and features.

The quantitative research approach was suitable for this study as it allowed the researchers to systematically gather measurable and standardized information from the intended participants. The results provided the foundation for determining user needs and aiding in the analysis, design, and creation of the suggested PasaHERO web-based commuter assistance system.



Flow of the Study

The project was carried out in several phases, beginning with the requirements stage, where the system requirements were divided into functional and non-functional requirements. 

The analysis phase focused on the data processing flow, UI/UX design, security and privacy, data storage, reporting tools, and user documentation to establish the overall system requirements and functionality. 

These were then translated into the design phase, where data modeling, Figma prototypes, system architecture, security framework, dashboard design, and user manuals were developed to serve as the blueprint for the application. 

The system then underwent a comprehensive testing phase consisting of system testing, which evaluated functionality, reliability, efficiency, security, route recommendation accuracy, and fare calculation accuracy, along with user testing involving students, daily commuters, first-time travelers, and tourists. 

The resulting system was PasaHERO: Multimodal AI Navigation with Jeepney Route Visualization and Fare Analytics in Cebu City, developed using a unified JavaScript technology stack consisting of HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, JavaScript (ES6+), Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, HTML5 Geolocation API, Node.js, Express.js, Sequelize ORM, and MySQL.  

Finally, the review phase assessed the system based on user satisfaction, route recommendation accuracy, fare calculation accuracy, navigation effectiveness, system performance, and overall acceptability to determine the quality and effectiveness of the developed application.

AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine 

To provide a clearer understanding of how the proposed PasaHERO system generates route recommendations, the researchers designed the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine shown in Figure 7. The engine serves as the decision-support component of the proposed system, responsible for evaluating multiple travel-related factors before recommending the most appropriate jeepney route. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination, after which the system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database. 















































Figure 7: AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine Architecture 

Figure 7 illustrates the architecture of the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine employed in the proposed PasaHERO system. The recommendation process begins when the commuter enters the current location and intended destination. The system retrieves relevant information from the GPS and map services, jeepney route database, fare database, and landmark database before evaluating multiple travel-related factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assessed using predefined recommendation criteria and assigned a recommendation score based on its overall suitability for the commuter's trip. Routes that satisfy favorable travel conditions, such as shorter walking distance, lower estimated fare, fewer transfers, greater route availability, shorter travel distance, and better landmark accessibility, receive higher recommendation scores and are prioritized by the system. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked route is presented together with the corresponding fare estimate, walking directions, boarding and drop-off locations, landmark-based guidance, and a geofenced "Para" notification. This recommendation process enables the system to provide context-aware route recommendations that improve the convenience, accessibility, and overall commuting experience of jeepney passengers in Cebu City.

Program Workflow

This section describes the program workflow of the proposed PasaHERO system. It illustrates the sequence of user interactions and automated system processes that work together to provide intelligent public transportation navigation in Cebu City. The workflow demonstrates how user inputs, real-time location data, jeepney route information, landmark records, and fare data are processed by the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine to generate multimodal route recommendations, interactive route visualizations, fare estimates, and navigation assistance. It also illustrates how the application continuously monitors the commuter's location to provide destination notifications ("Para" alerts) upon approaching the designated drop-off point. The PasaHERO program workflow consists of four interconnected workflows: the Overall Program Workflow, User Workflow, System Workflow, and Admin Workflow, each representing the major operational processes that support the functionality and maintenance of the proposed system. 



Figure 9: Detailed Program Workflow

Illustrates the overall operation of the PasaHERO application, beginning with user authentication and ending with trip completion. After logging into the system, commuters provide their current location through GPS or manual input and enter their intended destination. The system retrieves the necessary route, fare, and landmark information before the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine evaluates multiple contextual travel factors, including walking distance, estimated fare, route availability, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks. Each feasible route is assigned a recommendation score based on predefined recommendation criteria. The available routes are then ranked according to their recommendation scores, and the highest-ranked multimodal route is selected as the recommended route for the commuter.

The selected route is presented through an interactive map displaying jeepney routes, walking paths, boarding and drop-off points, nearby landmarks, estimated fares, and estimated travel time. During navigation, the application continuously tracks the commuter's real-time location, monitors route progress, and evaluates geofence boundaries to trigger a Destination Notification ("Para" Alert) when the commuter is approaching the designated drop-off location. Once the destination is reached, the system generates a trip summary containing the recommended route taken, estimated fare, travel distance, and travel time, saves the trip history in the database, and allows commuters to optionally provide feedback regarding their travel experience before completing the trip.

 



Figure 10: User Workflow

The User Workflow illustrates how commuters interact with the PasaHERO application throughout their journey. The workflow begins when users access the application and either log in to an existing account or register a new one. After authentication, the system verifies the user's credentials before directing them to the user dashboard. Commuters then set their trip details by providing their current location through GPS auto-detection or manual input and entering their intended destination. The system subsequently generates and displays an AI-powered recommended route that includes the appropriate jeepney route and signboard, first-mile and last-mile walking paths, estimated fare and travel time, boarding and drop-off points, and nearby landmarks. Users then start navigation and receive real-time route guidance, live location updates, and walking directions throughout the trip. As the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point, the application sends a geofenced "PARA" notification to remind the user to prepare for alighting. Once the destination is reached, the system presents a trip summary containing the route taken, estimated fare, distance traveled, and travel time. Users may optionally provide feedback by rating their travel experience before ending their session by logging out of the application. 



Figure 11: System Workflow

The System Workflow describes the internal operations performed by the proposed PasaHERO system after receiving a commuter's travel request. The process begins when the user provides the current location and destination, after which the system validates the input and retrieves the necessary data from the MySQL database, including jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, landmarks and stops, and LTFRB-approved fare rates. The AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine then evaluates all feasible routes by considering multiple contextual travel factors such as walking distance, estimated fare, route availability and frequency, number of transfers, travel distance, and nearby landmarks and safety. Each route is assigned a recommendation score and ranked according to its overall suitability before the highest-ranked route is selected and presented through an interactive map showing the jeepney route, boarding and drop-off points, walking directions, estimated fare, estimated travel time, and nearby landmarks. During the trip, the system continuously tracks the commuter's location using GPS to provide real-time navigation and automatically sends a geofenced "PARA" notification when the commuter approaches the designated drop-off point. Upon reaching the destination, the system generates a trip summary containing the route taken, fare, travel time, and trip date, allows the commuter to submit feedback, and stores the completed trip in the MySQL database for future reference. 





Figure 12: Admin Workflow

The Admin Workflow illustrates the administrative processes responsible for maintaining the accuracy, reliability, and efficiency of the proposed PasaHERO system. The workflow begins when the administrator securely logs into the admin dashboard using authorized credentials, where system statistics and recent activities are displayed. From the dashboard, the administrator manages jeepney routes, route codes and signboards, LTFRB fare rates, landmarks, jeepney stops, and user accounts to ensure that all navigation data remains accurate and up to date. The administrator also monitors reports and analytics, reviews trip records and statistics, manages announcements and notifications, configures system settings and user permissions, and performs database backup, restoration, maintenance, and integrity checks to preserve data reliability. All administrative updates are securely stored in the MySQL database, allowing the latest information to be reflected throughout the application and ensuring commuters receive accurate route recommendations, fare information, and real-time navigation services before the administrator securely logs out of the system.

Database Design

Entity-Relationship Diagram

An Entity Relationship Diagram (ERD) is a graphical representation that illustrates the relationships among the different entities and their corresponding interactions within a database system. For this study, the ERD serves as a blueprint for designing the database of the PasaHERO system by identifying the essential data entities, their attributes, and the relationships between them. It enables the researchers to organize data efficiently, maintain data integrity, and ensure that the system supports the required functionalities such as AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare computation, landmark-based guidance, and commuter notifications. The ERD also provides a structured foundation for database implementation, allowing the application to operate accurately, efficiently, and reliably. 





Figure 28: Entity Relationship Diagram

          Figure 28 illustrates the database structure of the proposed PasaHERO system. The database is centered on the USERS, TRIPS, and JEEPNEY_ROUTES entities, which collectively support commuter registration, trip planning, and intelligent route recommendation. Each user can create multiple trips, while each trip is associated with a selected jeepney route and is composed of one or more TRIP_SEGMENTS that represent the multimodal journey, including walking and jeepney travel. The JEEPNEY_ROUTES entity is linked to STOPS through the ROUTE_STOPS table, allowing the system to maintain the sequence of boarding and drop-off locations. Nearby LANDMARKS are associated with stops to provide landmark-based navigation and commuter guidance. The FARE_MATRIX entity stores the LTFRB-approved distance-based fare information used to estimate transportation costs, while the NOTIFICATIONS entity manages destination notifications ("Para" alerts) and other trip-related updates. The FEEDBACK entity stores commuter ratings and comments after each completed trip. Collectively, these entities establish a normalized relational database implemented in MySQL, providing reliable data management for user information, route visualization, fare computation, trip history, notifications, and the AI-Based Route Recommendation Engine that powers the PasaHERO system.

Technology Stack 

	PasaHERO consists of a web-based commuter assistance platform designed to help users navigate public transportation routes within Cebu City through AI-powered route recommendations, jeepney route visualization, fare estimation, and real-time commuter guidance. To ensure efficient system performance, responsive user interaction, and reliable processing of transportation data, the development process utilizes a modern technology stack that supports scalable web application development, dynamic user interfaces, and secure database management. 





Figure 30: Server Technology Stack

            		Figure XX illustrates the technology stack utilized in the development of PasaHERO, which adopts a full-stack web architecture composed of frontend, backend, database, mapping, intelligent route processing, and deployment technologies. The frontend is developed using HTML5, CSS3, Tailwind CSS, and JavaScript (ES6+) to provide a responsive and user-friendly interface. The backend is powered by Node.js and Express.js, which handle server-side processing, RESTful API development, authentication, and communication between the application and the database. MySQL serves as the relational database management system, while Sequelize ORM simplifies database interactions and manages relationships among users, jeepney routes, stops, landmarks, fare matrices, trips, notifications, and reports. For mapping and navigation, the system integrates Mapbox GL JS, Mapbox Directions API, and the HTML5 Geolocation API to support interactive route visualization, first-mile and last-mile guidance, real-time location tracking, and geofenced destination alerts. PasaHERO also incorporates a rule-based route recommendation engine and LTFRB distance-based fare engine to generate route recommendations and fare estimates based on predefined transportation data and fare rules. The system is deployed through cloud hosting with CDN and load-balancing support to ensure accessibility, reliability, and performance.
